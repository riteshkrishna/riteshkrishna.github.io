Hello ! My name is [Ritesh Krishna](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ritesh-krishna-phd-3875a332/) and I have created this space to write about my experiences in computing, science and technology. I am a Computer Scientist with a career history in industry and academia. For alomst a decade, I have been a member of IBM Research, co-located at the [Hartree Super-Computing Centre](https://www.hartree.stfc.ac.uk) in the UK.


The name of this blog - **360&deg; Computing** reflects my own views about computing, where every application that we see, has multiple layers beneath, going all the way to the first principles and hardware. In the era of *Apps*, it is easy to miss out on the underlying complexities that shape today's compute ecosystem. Cloud technologies and relatively easier programming languages (yes, Python - I am talking about you! 😄) have democratized computing, and we are seeing absolutely exciting applications from different domains. The ***effeciency*** and ***sustainability***, however, remains an art. This is even more relevant with the current costing structure, where the expenses are in form of resource utilization, API usage, tokens cost etc. The current AI advancements, particularly around Foundation Models, including LLMs, not only require better understanding of algorithms and infrastructure, but also benefit from better ***explainability*** through algorithms, as well as, the domain driven insights. I believe that the best form of computing is when we are able to most beneficially utilize every CPU/GPU cycle available to us - through skills that cut through the multiple strands of knowledge. We are seeing both theoretical and engineering advances, and it is the healthy mix of the two that creates a wholesome, powerful computing experience. The team composition and structure, particularly in interdisciplinary settings, comprising of scientists and engineers, also plays an important role in defining what can be achieved with what degree of success. I am hoping to write about some of my work and experiences at this site.
