Hello ! My name is [Ritesh Krishna](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ritesh-krishna-phd-3875a332/) and I have created this personal blog to write about my experiences in computing, science and technology. I am a Computer Scientist with a career history in industry and academia. For alomst a decade, I have been a member of IBM Research, co-located at the [Hartree Super-Computing Centre](https://www.hartree.stfc.ac.uk) in the UK.


The name of this blog - **360&deg; Computing** reflects my own views about computing, where every application that we see, has multiple layers beneath, going all the way to the first principles and hardware. In the era of *Apps*, it is easy to miss out on the underlying complexities that shape today's compute ecosystem. Cloud technologies and relatively easier programming languages (yes, Python - I am talking about you! 😄) have democratized computing across domains and new frameworks are emerging at breakneck speed. The current AI advancements, particularly around Foundation Models, including LLMs, are shaping new ways of doing things. Among all that, *effeciency* and *sustainability*, however, require wood-working. The AI outputs require *explainability* through algorithms, as well as, the domain driven insights. I am hoping to write about some of these things in this space, hopefully, in a simpler to digest form.

I am also passionate about creating a balance between theoretical and engineering activities, as it is the healthy mix of the two that creates a wholesome, powerful computing experience. The team composition and structure, particularly in interdisciplinary settings, comprising of scientists and engineers,  play an important role in defining what can be achieved, and the users of a technology define what success means. The 360 is a mix of all the above and more, I am hoping to touch on these subjects based on my experience. 

The **360** also has an historic IBM connection and you can read about it [here](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IBM_System/360)
